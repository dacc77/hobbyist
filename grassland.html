Grasslands of California

Grasslands are defined as large open areas where the vegetation is predominantly grasses. Around the globe, they cover appoximately 1/3 of the Earth's surface. They only recieve limited annual rainfall and have a rather dry climate with low amount of nutrients in the soil. California's grasslands are spread throughout our Great Central Valley. It is more commonly known as Redding, Chico, Modesto, Merced, Bakersfield and other surrounding cities. Grasslands are currently in critical condition. In its original state, it was one of the most diverse and distinctive glasslands of North America.
