Kelp Forests of California

California kelp forests are underwater ecosytems characterized by dense kelp plants along the state's coast. It's home to species like the horned shark, sea otters, bat rays, sea urchins and our state fish, the Garabaldi. The kelp plants themselves are fast-growing often reaching over 30 meters as they absorb sunlight and nutrients from the ocean. These forests are severely affected by pollution, destructive fishing practices, overfishing and coastal development.
