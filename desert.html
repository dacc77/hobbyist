Deserts of California

A desert is an area that recieves very little rain. Anuually, Californian deserts recieve an average of 7 inches of rainfall per year. A few of the deserts within California include the Colorado Desert, Mojave Desert and Sonoran. Despite there being limited rainfall, its still home to animals such as rattlesnakes, hares, kangaroo rats, tortoises and others. Like several other animals, tortoises in the mojave desert have had their population decrease by 90% due to habitat destruction, vandalism, ubranization and solar farms.
