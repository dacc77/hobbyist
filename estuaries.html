Estuaries of California

Estuaries are areas where freshwater meets salty ocean water resulting in brackish water. Various habitats such as tidal pools, mangrove forests, seagrass beds, swamps are found in or around estuaries which provide a unique environment for wildlife that rely on these habitats to live, feed and reproduce. Due to these ideal spawning places, migratory birds often depend on these areas to feed. As a result of urbanization, estuaries in areas such as San Francisco, Morro Bay and Santa Monica Bay are now threatened.
